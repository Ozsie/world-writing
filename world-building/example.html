Story

Night fell over the city of glass, and the wind hummed through the sky-bridges like a distant choir. Lanterns blinked awake one by one, each a tiny comet trapped in a bottle. Below, the river stitched black silk between the districts, carrying whispers from one shore to the other.

Mara stood at the edge of Bridge Seven, clutching a letter sealed in violet wax. The seal bore the sigil of the Astral Cartographers—a compass rose encircled by seven stars. She had memorized the constellation years ago, but tonight the pattern felt less like a map and more like a key. Somewhere in this tessellation of glass and light, a door was waiting.

“If the world is a clock,” her mentor once said, “then the secret city is the second hand. You don’t see it until you listen.”

She closed her eyes. The bridge’s hum split into harmonics. Beneath them, the quiet tick of hidden gears. The city was speaking.
